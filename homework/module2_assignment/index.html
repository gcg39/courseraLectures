Types of rocks

Igneous Rocks

Igneous rocks refer to the upcoming magma wich cools down and solidifies when reaching the atmosphere. Even the terms comes form the latin root "ignis", meaning fire or related to it. Some examples are:

  • Granite.
  • Basalt.
  • Gabbro.
  • Diorite.
  • Andesite.

Sedimentary Rocks

Sedimentary rocks are formed form debris or former rocks, mineral or organic particles, like sand or gravel. As time pass by these particles form litified strata and eventually they might reach the surface again with a little help of isostasy and the curst's movement. Bellow there are some examples:

  • Sandstone.
  • Claystone.
  • Siltstone.
  • Shale.

Metamorphic Rocks

These are rocks lying deep down the Earth's crust which, due to the stress and high temperatures they are subjected to, change in shape, colour, and even chemical composition. Some examples are:

  • Amphibolite.
  • Gneiss.
  • Horngels.
  • Margble.
